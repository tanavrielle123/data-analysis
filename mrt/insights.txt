Transport Peak Hour Analysis – Insights

1. Passenger demand shows two clear peak periods:
   - Morning peak around 8am
   - Evening peak around 6pm

2. Midday demand is significantly lower, suggesting reduced commuter activity.

3. This indicates a predictable commuting pattern where most passengers travel before and after work or school hours.

4. Such patterns could help transport planners optimise train frequency, staffing allocation, and maintenance scheduling.


Observations:

📈 Morning spike around 8am (~9000 passengers)
📉 Big drop late morning (~10–12pm ~2500–3000)
📈 Gradual rise again afternoon
📈 Even bigger spike at 6pm (~9500)
📉 Drop again night

This suggests:

1. Commuter Behaviour Pattern
- People travel to work/school in morning
- Return home after work in evening
- This creates bi-modal peak pattern.

2. Operational Insight (Transport planners would infer):
- trains must be more frequent at peak
- staff deployment should match peak windows
- maintenance should avoid peak hours
